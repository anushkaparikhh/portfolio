Personal Contributions

I worked with the design team to create brainstorm and narrow down a cohesive design system that would be the most effecient for astronaut use. Once a design system was solidified, our team was split into groups that focused on specific tasks to prototype the interface. My group mainly focused on geo sampling/terrain scanning and PR/EV task lists. I worked on a team of four designers, including me, Anika Gupta, Ava Maghsoodlou, and Bennett Graff.

Drafts for the design system.

Key Points of Interest for Geological Sampling

Geological sampling consisted of four main tasks. The first was to organize PR and EV tasks in a way that would reveal all important information, such as the time appropriate for the task and location. Another key part of our role was to find a system to organize the tags given to each geological sample to effectively identify/categorize samples. Additionally, we had to find a way to communicate warnings between EV and PR tasks that would catch attention of the astronaut while also remaining informative.

Research and Prototyping

Our research took much inspiration from previous years in NASA S.U.I.T.S. as well as navigation flows and Crew Dragon touchscreen displays. Google Maps also served as guidance of how to arrange tasks/directions to users.
Thus, we collected all our research to create lo-fi designs that were developed after solidifying a design system to high-fi wireframes.

Iterations

Through testing, our team discovered that creating the vanishing of the warning signal confused the user as to whether the warning had been fixed or was still active. This meant a more thoughtful approach would be needed to address the state of a warning after being acknowledged, resolved, or unresolved.

Problem

This creates confusion as to whether the warning has been resolved when switching between EV and PR tasks.

Solution

This considers both states with the first image displaying the inital warning signal and the second image showing that the warnings have been read but are not resolved.
